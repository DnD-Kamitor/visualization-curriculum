10  Session 3 - Lesser Evils

Central Dilemma: Is a “lesser evil” still evil? How far do you compromise?

Session Goal: Give the players three real alternative paths, each with genuine moral costs. Force them to stop looking for a clean solution.

GM Principle for This Session: Every alternative is worse than it sounds. Every alternative is better than it sounds. That’s the point.

10.1 Pre-Session Checklist

10.2 The Time Pressure Mechanic

Beginning this session, the clock becomes explicit.

Introduce this at the session’s start, as a concrete event:

10.2.1 Read-Aloud (Opening)

Tomas Areth finds you before you find him. He’s been doing math.

“The grey sickness rate has been accelerating,” he says, without greeting. “I asked the healers to let me see the numbers. Not the public numbers - the actual numbers.” He spreads a hand-drawn chart on the table. “Twelve people a month in the first decade. Eighteen two years ago. Twenty-six last month.” He lets you look at it. “If this curve holds, we lose forty percent of the city’s productive adult population in eleven years. The city itself follows about three years later.”

He folds the chart.

“I’m telling you this because I think you’re trying to find an alternative to the ritual. I want you to succeed. And I want you to know what the clock actually looks like while you’re trying.”

Mechanical Effect: Players have approximately two more sessions before the Living World consequences become irreversible. At the start of every subsequent session, remind them of this with a brief update.

10.3 The Three Alternative Paths

Each path is a real option. None of them are perfect. Present all three - through different NPCs, different investigative threads, different sources - and let the players choose.

10.3.1 Path 1: The Partial Ritual - “Three Are Enough”

Source: Theron Waide, if they have fully won his trust.

What It Is: An incomplete reading of the ritual texts suggests that concentrated rather than distributed extinguishing might work. Instead of all ten Lux Anchors, perhaps three with the strongest connections - Sera, Tomas, and one other - might provide enough energy to trigger the sun’s return.

The Moral Cost: This is speculation. Theron cannot verify it without the destroyed section of Corven’s notes. There is approximately a 40% chance (in his estimation) that three deaths would accomplish nothing, and the remaining seven Lux Anchors would be permanently destabilized.

Also: three people still die.

NPC Reactions: - Sera: “If it’s verified, I’d do it. But I won’t die on a maybe.” - Tomas: “The arithmetic is appealing. The uncertainty is not. I’d want three independent confirmations before I’d recommend this path.” - Lira: “You came to me with ‘it might only be three of us.’ That’s supposed to be good news?”

Key Decision Point: Do the players pursue verification, which will take time and may reveal the partial ritual is impossible? Or do they act on the possibility?

10.3.2 Path 2: The Willing Few - “Consent Changes the Calculus”

Source: Brother Edoran has already been quietly asking. Some of the Dawnborn have already answered.

What It Is: Three of the Dawnborn have privately indicated - to Edoran, not to the players - that they would consent to the ritual if it were voluntary. Ysel Maren (the temple keeper), Davin Shore (the retired soldier), and Cormac Drell (the dock master) are the willing ones. Seven are not.

The Moral Cost: Three willing deaths doesn’t complete the ritual. The math requires ten. Even if the willing three proceed, seven more are needed - and they haven’t consented.

But: if the willing three go first, and the ritual begins, does the momentum change? Does seeing the sun start to return change the unwilling seven’s calculus? Or does it trap them?

NPC Reactions: - Edoran: “I am not asking for the unwilling ones. I am asking for the willing ones’ right to choose.” - Ysel Maren (if players meet her directly): “This is my calling. I have never been more certain of anything. Please don’t try to talk me out of it.” - Lira (on hearing about the willing three): “Good for them. That doesn’t change my answer.” - Sera: “If Davin and Ysel are willing, I can’t tell them no. But I’m not there yet myself.”

Key Decision Point: Do the players facilitate the willing deaths? Do they try to use the partial ritual as cover? And once they start, can they stop?

10.3.3 Path 3: The Transfer - “What If the Energy Could Move?”

Source: A Spire Quarter mage named Isolde Menth who has been quietly studying the Dawnborn for years without their full understanding of what she was looking at.

What It Is: Isolde believes - and this is theory, not certainty - that the Lux Anchor energy might be transferable. If the energy could be extracted from the Dawnborn and placed into a non-living anchor, the ritual might proceed without killing anyone.

The Moral Cost: The extraction process is dangerous and untested. In Isolde’s estimate, each extraction has a 20-30% chance of killing the Dawnborn anyway. And the anchor vessel would need to be exactly right - she believes only something within the original ritual’s sympathetic resonance would work.

She has a theory about what that vessel might be.

It is the Auris Cathedral.

The Cathedral has stood for 200 years. It is the city’s most important religious and cultural site. It would be destroyed in the process.

NPC Reactions: - Tomas: “Destroy the Cathedral to save the Dawnborn. The Auris clergy will fight that to the last person.” - The Auris clergy (one faction, split): Half refuse on principle. Half are the faction that believes the god’s suffering is in the Cathedral’s continued function. They might be moved. - Lira: “Yes. This. Find a way to make this work.” - Edoran: “Fascinating. Tell me - what happens if the transfer fails and three Dawnborn die and the Cathedral is destroyed?”

Key Decision Point: Do they pursue the transfer? Do they involve Isolde fully, risking that she may want credit (and political power) for the solution? Do they tell the Auris clergy what it will cost?

10.4 NPC Relationships: Deepening and Fracturing

This session is when relationships become stakes.

10.4.1 Sera and Lira

They have known each other for fifty years. They have different answers. This session, they have their first real argument - in front of the players, if the players have been present in both their lives.

Sera: “Someone has to make a decision. We can’t just wait for a perfect answer that might not exist.”

Lira: “I have a daughter.”

A silence.

Sera: (Quietly.) “Oh.”

This is the moment Lira’s secret comes out - at least to Sera, and to players present. Handle it gently. It doesn’t resolve anything. It makes everything harder.

10.4.2 Tomas and the Chancellor

Tomas has requested a private meeting with Chancellor Ostenveld. She agreed. Players may learn about this, attend it (with appropriate diplomacy), or be told about it afterward.

The substance of the meeting: Tomas is proposing a formal timeline. If a solution isn’t found in X days, he will publicly announce the situation to the city - the ritual’s cost, the Dawnborn’s nature, all of it. He believes the people have the right to know. The Chancellor believes the announcement will cause a riot.

They are both right.

GM Tip: This is the players’ cue. They have limited time before the situation becomes public, uncontrollable, or forced. Tomas is not bluffing.

10.4.3 Edoran and Ysel

The players may witness - or be told of - Brother Edoran meeting with Ysel Maren at the Auris Cathedral. It is not a secret meeting. They are both very calm. They are discussing the timing of the willing Dawnborn’s ritual.

If the players intervene: Edoran asks them what their plan is. He is genuinely asking - not confrontational. “I’m not doing this because I enjoy it. I’m doing it because someone needs to, and no one else has a better plan. Show me a better plan.”

If the players don’t intervene: The willing three have quietly begun preparing.

10.5 The Combat Option: A Desperate Act

Use this if the session needs a kinetic break, or if the Living World has escalated.

What Happens: A mob from the Lowmark - a group of the Desperate - attempts to seize three of the Dawnborn. They don’t want to kill them. They want to make them do the ritual. They have heard the rumors. They are terrified. They have concluded that if the Dawnborn won’t choose, the choice will be made for them.

This is not a villain attack. These are suffering people making a terrible decision. The leader of the mob is a woman named Senna Kard who lost her husband to grey sickness six months ago. She is not evil. She is desperate.

The players must de-escalate or engage. If they fight, they are fighting starving workers. If they negotiate, they have to offer something real - not just platitudes.

Senna’s Demand: “Give us an answer. A real one. A date. Tell us the sun comes back before my children go grey, and I’ll take these people home.”

Can the players make that promise?

10.6 Closing Beat

End this session with the players having committed - or explicitly declined to commit - to at least one path.

10.6.1 Read-Aloud

The alternatives are on the table. All of them have costs. All of them have prices that someone will pay, whether you counted on it or not.

The question of which evil is lesser depends entirely on who you ask. Ask Lira: the one that lets her live. Ask Ysel: the one that lets her choose. Ask Senna: the one that saves her children. Ask Tomas: the one we can all live with afterward.

Ask the city: the one that works.

You are somewhere in the middle of all those answers, and the clock Tomas showed you keeps moving.

10.6.2 Post-Session Question

Ask the players directly: “If all the choices are bad, do you still have to choose? And if you don’t choose - is that also a choice?”

10.7 Session 3 Consequence Tracking

Outcome Note
Which path(s) are being pursued? Record all active investigations
Does Lira’s daughter secret expand? Note who knows
Did the mob scene happen? What was the outcome? Note Senna’s status and the Desperate faction level
Did Tomas deliver his ultimatum to the Chancellor? Note the deadline he set
Have the willing Dawnborn begun preparing? If yes, note timeline
Is Isolde Menth being investigated? Note her trust level
